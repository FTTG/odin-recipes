Hambagu Steak

Description

The Hambagu Steak is an interesting take on a German dish that took a twist when it was first brought over to Japan. Delicious ground beef mixed with vegetables and panko (japanese breadcrumbs), pan fried and served with a delicious sweet sauce and vegetables.

Ingredients

For the Hambagu Patties

  • 1/2 onion
  • 1 Tbsp neutral oil (canola, vegetable, etc.)
  • 3/4 ground beef and pork combination (A good mix would be 2 to 1 beef to pork)
  • 1/2 tsb kosher salt
  • freshly grounded black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/3 cup panko
  • 2 Tbsp milk
  • 1 large egg

For Cooking the Hambagu

  • 1 Tbsp neutral-flavoured oil
  • 3 Tbsp red wine for steaming (can substitute with beef/chicken stock)

For the Sauce

  • 1 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • 3 Tbsp tonkatsu sauce (can substitute with Worcestershire sauce with sugar)
  • 3 Tbsp red wine
  • 3 Tbsp water

Instructions

Making the Hambagu Patties

  1. Cut onions into small cubes
  2. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat and saute the onions until tender and almost translucent. Place them on a bowl
  3. Once onions are cool, add beef and prok to the bowl. Add the salt and black pepper to taste, and the nutmeg
  4. Add the milk, panko, and egg
  5. Mix it all together with a spatula or a spoon
  6. Keep mixing by hand until its sticky and pale
  7. Divide the mixture into quarters and scoop each up for each portion
  8. Toss each portion between your hands to release any air inside the mixture
  9. Lastly, shape them into an oval-shapped patty and place them on a tray. Cover the patties with plastic wrap and refrigerate them for at least 30minutes before cooking, so that the fat solidifies

To Cook the Hambagu

  1. In a large pan, heat oil over medium heat and place patties gently into the pan. Indent the center of each patty with to fingers, since the center will rise with the heat.
  2. Cook each patty until brown on the bottom, for about 3 minutes. Then flip and cook for another 3 minutes until browned.
  3. Add red wine for steaming the patties and reduce heat to low. Cover the pan with a lid and cook for 5-7 minutes to cook the inside of the patties.
  4. Test if patties are done by inserting a skewer; if clear juice comes out, it's done. Increase heat to medium again to let alcohol finish evaporating. Transfer the steaks to a plate. Do not wash the pan as you will use it for the sauce using the pan juices.

To make the Sauce

  1. In the same frying pan, add the unsalted butter, ketchup, and tonkatsu sauce.
  2. Add the water and red wine for the sauce to the pan.
  3. Mix well together and bring sauce to a simmer over medium heat; let the alcohol evaporate.
  4. When the sauce thickens (test by checking if you can draw a line at the bottom of the pan with a spatula), remove from the heat. Drizzle the sauce over the Hambagu steak. Save any leftover sauce in a bowl to use later or if anyone wants more! You can serve the steak with steamed rice, miso soup, and steamed vegetables or a salad.
